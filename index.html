Maui Wildfire Recovery as a Spatial Externality Game

This interactive map visualizes a game theory model of wildfire recovery and rebuilding decisions in Maui following the 2023 wildfires. The goal of the model is to illustrate how individual property owners decide whether to comply with wildfire mitigation requirements, and how those decisions may depend on risk, enforcement, and costs.

The Problem

After a major wildfire, property owners often face rebuilding rules designed to reduce future wildfire risk. These rules might include requirements such as defensible space, fire-resistant materials, or vegetation management. Complying with these rules can reduce wildfire risk, but compliance also has financial costs.

Because these costs are borne individually, property owners may choose not to comply unless the benefits outweigh the costs. However, wildfire mitigation also creates externalities: when one property reduces its fire risk, nearby properties may also become safer.

This creates a strategic situation where each property owner must decide whether to comply while considering risk, enforcement, and costs.

The Model

The model evaluates whether compliance becomes the dominant strategy using the following condition:

Lθ + pF > c

Where:

  • L = expected wildfire loss if no mitigation occurs
  • θ = effectiveness of mitigation at reducing wildfire damage
  • p = probability that non-compliance is detected
  • F = penalty imposed for non-compliance
  • c = cost of complying with mitigation requirements

The left side of the equation represents the expected benefits of compliance. Mitigation reduces expected wildfire loss, and enforcement creates an expected penalty for failing to comply.

If these expected benefits exceed the cost of compliance, then compliance becomes the rational strategy.

How the Map Works

The map shows three example locations in Maui: Lahaina, Waiohuli, and Kahului. Each location is represented by a small 3×3 grid intented to represent a neighborhood.

When you click a grid, controls appear that allow you to adjust the parameters of the model. As the parameters change, the model recalculates the equilibrium condition and updates the grid colors:

  • Green cells indicate that compliance is the equilibrium strategy.
  • Red cells indicate that non-compliance is the equilibrium strategy.

By adjusting the sliders, you can explore how wildfire risk, enforcement probability, penalties, and mitigation costs influence the strategic incentives faced by property owners.

Purpose of the Visualization

This visualization is not intended to predict real-world outcomes. Instead, it serves as an exploratory tool that demonstrates how economic incentives, risk perception, and enforcement policies can influence recovery decisions after a wildfire.
